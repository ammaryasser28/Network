## ğŸ”Œ 2. Coaxial Cable

- A **central copper conductor** surrounded by an insulator and metallic shield.  
- Resistant to interference compared to twisted pair.  
- Used in older **TV networks and LANs**.  
- Lower speed and distance limitations compared to UTP/STP.

## âš¡ 3. Fiber Optic Cables

- Transmits data using **light through glass or plastic fibers**.  
- Advantages:
  - Very high speed (10 Gbps+).  
  - Long-distance transmission without signal loss.  
  - Immune to electromagnetic interference.  
- Types:
  - **Single-mode Fiber (SMF)**: long-distance, narrow beam.  
  - **Multi-mode Fiber (MMF)**: shorter distance, wider beam, lower cost.
